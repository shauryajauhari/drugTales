This pipeline, as illustrated below, shall work in tandem (in the background) to fetch the contemporary conversations on the medicines, and amalgamate the information to the DrugTales platform, thus ameliorating its dexterity.

We came across a relevant publication in this category- PeopleSave. The paper “PeopleSave: Recommending effective drugs through web crowdsourcing” introduces a system that leverages patient reviews from the internet to recommend effective drugs. By collecting and filtering reviews from various online sources, PeopleSave ensures the reliability of the information and analyzes it contextually to understand patient-reported outcomes. The system’s architecture includes modules for data collection, filtering, contextual analysis, and recommendation generation, using advanced algorithms to process natural language. The evaluation shows that PeopleSave’s recommendations align well with actual patient outcomes, demonstrating the potential of web crowdsourcing in enhancing drug recommendation accuracy and relevance. The study highlights the benefits and challenges of this approach, suggesting future improvements for more patient-centered healthcare.
